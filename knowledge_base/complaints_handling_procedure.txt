COMPLAINTS HANDLING PROCEDURE

INITIAL COMPLAINT RECEPTION
===========================

Receiving Complaints
- Listen actively without interrupting the customer
- Express empathy: "I understand your concern" and "Let me help resolve this"
- Remain calm and professional regardless of customer's tone
- Take detailed notes throughout the conversation
- Avoid making promises until investigation is complete

Complaint Logging Requirements
- Record complaint in CRM system immediately
- Assign unique complaint reference number
- Document customer contact information and order details
- Note date, time, and method of complaint (phone, email, chat)
- Record representative name handling the complaint

Initial Information Gathering
- Customer name, phone number, and email address
- Order number and purchase date
- Product(s) involved in complaint
- Detailed description of the issue
- Photos or documentation if available
- Desired resolution from customer's perspective

Immediate Acknowledgment
- Provide complaint reference number to customer
- Confirm complaint details with customer
- Set expectations for investigation timeline
- Provide direct contact information for follow-up
- Send email confirmation within 1 hour

COMPLAINT CLASSIFICATION
========================

Severity Levels

Level 1 - Critical (Immediate Response Required)
- Food safety concerns or suspected illness
- Product contamination or foreign objects
- Severe allergic reactions
- Product temperature abuse during shipping
- Emergency situations requiring immediate action

Level 2 - High Priority (Same Day Response)
- Product quality issues (spoilage, off-taste, texture)
- Shipping damage or package compromise
- Incorrect products shipped
- Missing items from order
- Significant service failures

Level 3 - Standard Priority (24-48 Hour Response)
- Minor product quality concerns
- Shipping delays
- Website or ordering system issues
- Billing discrepancies
- General service improvement suggestions

Level 4 - Low Priority (3-5 Business Days)
- Packaging suggestions
- General feedback or comments
- Product availability inquiries
- Recipe or cooking questions
- Non-urgent account issues

Complaint Categories
- Product Quality (taste, texture, appearance, freshness)
- Food Safety (contamination, illness, temperature issues)
- Shipping/Delivery (delays, damage, temperature, packaging)
- Customer Service (representative behavior, response time)
- Billing/Payment (charges, refunds, payment processing)
- Website/Technology (ordering system, account access)
- Policy/Procedure (returns, refunds, terms of service)

INVESTIGATION PROCEDURES
========================

Immediate Investigation Steps
- Review order history and shipping records
- Check product lot numbers and batch information
- Verify shipping carrier tracking and temperature logs
- Review previous customer interactions and complaints
- Contact warehouse team for product handling information

Product Quality Investigations
- Request photos of product and packaging
- Arrange for product sample return if necessary
- Contact supplier for lot-specific information
- Review production and quality control records
- Coordinate with quality assurance team for testing

Food Safety Investigations
- Escalate immediately to Food Safety Manager
- Document all symptoms and timeline if illness reported
- Coordinate with health department if required
- Initiate product hold procedures for affected lots
- Contact legal team for serious incidents

Shipping/Delivery Investigations
- Contact shipping carrier for detailed tracking
- Review temperature monitoring data
- Check packaging integrity and insulation effectiveness
- Verify delivery address and signature requirements
- Coordinate with shipping department for carrier claims

Service-Related Investigations
- Review call recordings if available
- Interview involved representatives
- Check email correspondence history
- Verify policy interpretation and application
- Review training records of involved staff

RESOLUTION PROCEDURES
=====================

Resolution Options

Product Replacement
- Ship identical replacement product immediately
- Use expedited shipping at no charge
- Include gift or bonus item for inconvenience
- Provide tracking information and delivery confirmation
- Follow up to ensure satisfaction with replacement

Refund Processing
- Full refund for product and shipping costs
- Process refund to original payment method
- Provide refund confirmation and timeline
- Offer store credit option for faster processing
- Include goodwill gesture for significant issues

Service Recovery
- Formal apology from management for service failures
- Staff retraining when appropriate
- Process improvement implementation
- Customer account flagging for enhanced service
- Courtesy follow-up from supervisor

Preventive Measures
- Supplier notification and corrective action
- Product recall initiation if necessary
- Process changes to prevent recurrence
- Staff training updates
- System improvements or modifications

ESCALATION PROCEDURES
=====================

Internal Escalation Path
Level 1: Customer Service Representative
Level 2: Customer Service Supervisor
Level 3: Customer Service Manager
Level 4: Operations Director
Level 5: General Manager

Escalation Triggers
- Customer requests supervisor involvement
- Complaint involves potential legal liability
- Resolution requires policy exception
- Cost of resolution exceeds representative authority
- Multiple complaints about same issue/product

Department-Specific Escalations
- Food Safety Issues: Food Safety Manager (ext. 311)
- Quality Problems: Quality Assurance (ext. 315)
- Shipping Issues: Logistics Manager (ext. 302)
- Billing Disputes: Accounting Manager (ext. 203)
- Legal Concerns: Legal Department (ext. 501)

External Escalations
- Health Department notification for serious food safety issues
- Carrier notification for shipping-related problems
- Supplier notification for product quality issues
- Insurance company for liability claims
- Legal counsel for potential litigation

Management Authorization Required
- Refunds over $500
- Product recalls or holds
- Policy exceptions or waivers
- Public relations response needed
- Media or social media complaints

DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
===========================

Complaint File Contents
- Initial complaint description and classification
- All customer communications (emails, call notes)
- Investigation findings and evidence
- Resolution offered and customer response
- Follow-up communications and outcomes

Required Documentation
- Photos of products or packaging issues
- Shipping and tracking documentation
- Temperature monitoring data
- Product testing results if applicable
- Supplier or carrier correspondence

Record Retention
- Maintain complaint files for minimum 3 years
- Store documentation in both physical and digital formats
- Ensure confidentiality and secure access
- Regular backup of digital complaint records
- Legal review for litigation-sensitive complaints

Reporting Requirements
- Daily complaint summary to management
- Weekly trend analysis and reporting
- Monthly complaint metrics and resolution rates
- Quarterly complaint category analysis
- Annual complaint prevention effectiveness review

COMMUNICATION PROTOCOLS
========================

Customer Communication Standards
- Respond to initial complaint within 4 hours
- Provide investigation updates every 24-48 hours
- Use customer's preferred communication method
- Maintain professional and empathetic tone
- Confirm resolution satisfaction before closing

Internal Communication
- Notify supervisor of Level 1 complaints immediately
- Update management on significant complaint trends
- Coordinate with relevant departments for investigations
- Share complaint information for training purposes
- Report systemic issues for process improvement

Written Communication Guidelines
- Use company letterhead for formal correspondence
- Include complaint reference number in all communications
- Summarize issue, investigation, and resolution clearly
- Avoid admissions of fault or liability
- Copy appropriate managers on significant complaints

FOLLOW-UP PROCEDURES
====================

Post-Resolution Follow-Up
- Contact customer 3-5 days after resolution
- Confirm satisfaction with resolution
- Address any remaining concerns
- Gather feedback on complaint handling process
- Document follow-up outcome in complaint file

Customer Satisfaction Verification
- Use standard satisfaction survey questions
- Record satisfaction rating (1-5 scale)
- Note specific feedback or suggestions
- Identify opportunities for service improvement
- Report satisfaction trends to management

Long-Term Follow-Up
- Monitor customer's future orders and satisfaction
- Flag account for enhanced service attention
- Track resolution effectiveness over time
- Identify customers for potential retention outreach
- Use feedback for training and process improvement

PREVENTION AND ANALYSIS
=======================

Trend Analysis
- Weekly review of complaint categories and frequencies
- Monthly analysis of complaint root causes
- Quarterly review of prevention effectiveness
- Annual comprehensive complaint trend report
- Comparative analysis with industry benchmarks

Prevention Initiatives
- Supplier quality improvement programs
- Enhanced product testing and quality control
- Improved packaging and shipping procedures

- Staff training based on complaint patterns

Process Improvement
- Regular review and update of complaint procedures
- Customer feedback integration into service improvements
- Technology upgrades to improve complaint handling
- Cross-training of staff for better complaint resolution
- Preventive measure effectiveness monitoring

Ongoing Training

Performance Monitoring

LEGAL CONSIDERATIONS
====================

Liability Protection
- Avoid admissions of fault or responsibility
- Document factual information only
- Refer legal questions to legal department
- Maintain confidentiality of investigation details
- Follow company guidelines for public statements

Regulatory Compliance
- Report food safety incidents as required by law
- Maintain records for regulatory inspection
- Coordinate with USDA for serious food safety issues
- Follow state notification requirements
- Ensure compliance with consumer protection laws
