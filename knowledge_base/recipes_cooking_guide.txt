RECIPES & COOKING GUIDE

VENISON RECIPES
===============

Classic Venison Steaks
Ingredients:
- 4 venison steaks (6-8 oz each)
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp fresh rosemary, chopped
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- 2 tbsp butter

Instructions:
1. Remove steaks from refrigerator 30 minutes before cooking
2. Season both sides with salt, pepper, garlic, and rosemary
3. Heat olive oil in cast iron skillet over medium-high heat
4. Sear steaks 3-4 minutes per side for medium-rare (internal temp 135°F)
5. Add butter to pan in last minute, baste steaks
6. Rest 5 minutes before serving
Serving: Serve with roasted root vegetables and red wine reduction

Slow-Cooked Venison Stew
Ingredients:
- 2 lbs venison shoulder, cubed
- 2 tbsp flour
- 3 tbsp vegetable oil
- 1 large onion, diced
- 3 carrots, sliced
- 3 celery stalks, chopped
- 4 cups beef broth
- 2 cups red wine
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tsp thyme

Instructions:
1. Coat venison cubes in seasoned flour
2. Brown meat in oil in large pot over medium-high heat
3. Add vegetables and cook 5 minutes
4. Add broth, wine, and herbs
5. Bring to boil, reduce heat, simmer covered 2-3 hours until tender
6. Remove bay leaves before serving
Serving: Serve over mashed potatoes or with crusty bread

WILD BOAR RECIPES
=================

Herb-Crusted Wild Boar Tenderloin
Ingredients:
- 2 lbs wild boar tenderloin
- 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
- 1 cup fresh breadcrumbs
- 2 tbsp fresh thyme
- 2 tbsp fresh sage, chopped
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 3 tbsp olive oil

Instructions:
1. Preheat oven to 425°F
2. Season tenderloin with salt and pepper
3. Sear in hot skillet until browned on all sides
4. Brush with Dijon mustard
5. Mix breadcrumbs, herbs, garlic, and olive oil
6. Press herb mixture onto tenderloin
7. Roast 15-20 minutes until internal temp reaches 145°F
8. Rest 10 minutes before slicing
Serving: Serve with apple chutney and roasted Brussels sprouts

ELK RECIPES
===========

Grilled Elk Burgers
Ingredients:
- 1.5 lbs ground elk
- 1 small onion, finely minced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 egg
- 1/4 cup breadcrumbs
- 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce

Instructions:
1. Mix ground elk, onion, garlic, egg, breadcrumbs, and seasonings
2. Form into 6 patties
3. Grill over medium heat 4-5 minutes per side
4. Cook to internal temperature of 160°F
5. Let rest 2 minutes before serving
Serving: Serve with caramelized onions and sharp cheddar

BISON RECIPES
=============

Bison Ribeye with Coffee Rub
Ingredients:
- 4 bison ribeye steaks
- 2 tbsp ground coffee
- 1 tbsp brown sugar
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper

Instructions:
1. Mix all dry ingredients for rub
2. Coat steaks with rub, let sit 30 minutes
3. Grill over high heat 3-4 minutes per side for medium-rare
4. Internal temperature should reach 130°F
5. Rest 5 minutes before serving
Serving: Serve with grilled corn and chimichurri sauce

EXOTIC POULTRY RECIPES
======================

Pan-Seared Quail with Bacon
Ingredients:
- 4 whole quail, cleaned
- 8 strips bacon
- 2 tbsp butter
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 onion, sliced
- 1/2 cup white wine

Instructions:
1. Wrap each quail with 2 strips of bacon
2. Season with salt and pepper
3. Heat oil and butter in large skillet
4. Cook quail 4-5 minutes per side until golden
5. Add onions and wine, cover and simmer 10 minutes
6. Internal temperature should reach 165°F
Serving: Serve with wild rice pilaf

Roasted Duck with Orange Glaze
Ingredients:
- 1 whole duck (4-5 lbs)
- 1 orange, quartered
- 2 tbsp honey
- 1/4 cup orange juice
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated

Instructions:
1. Preheat oven to 325°F
2. Stuff duck cavity with orange quarters
3. Score skin in crosshatch pattern
4. Season with salt and pepper
5. Roast 20 minutes per pound
6. Mix honey, orange juice, soy sauce, and ginger for glaze
7. Brush with glaze every 30 minutes
8. Cook until internal temp reaches 165°F
Serving: Serve with roasted vegetables and orange sauce

SPECIALTY MEAT RECIPES
======================

Kangaroo Steaks with Pepper Sauce
Ingredients:
- 4 kangaroo steaks (6 oz each)
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp green peppercorns
- 1/4 cup brandy
- 1/2 cup heavy cream
- 2 tbsp butter

Instructions:
1. Season steaks with salt
2. Heat oil in skillet over high heat
3. Sear steaks 2-3 minutes per side for rare to medium-rare
4. Remove steaks and keep warm
5. Add peppercorns and brandy to pan
6. Add cream and simmer until thickened
7. Whisk in butter and serve over steaks
Serving: Serve with roasted sweet potato and asparagus

Grilled Alligator Tail
Ingredients:
- 2 lbs alligator tail meat, cubed
- 1/4 cup olive oil
- 2 tbsp lemon juice
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp paprika
- 1 tsp cayenne pepper

Instructions:
1. Marinate alligator in oil, lemon juice, garlic, and spices for 2 hours
2. Thread onto skewers
3. Grill over medium-high heat 3-4 minutes per side
4. Don't overcook - meat should be white and firm
5. Internal temperature should reach 145°F
Serving: Serve with spicy aioli and coleslaw

MARINADES & SEASONINGS
======================

Basic Game Meat Marinade
- 1/2 cup olive oil
- 1/4 cup red wine vinegar
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh herbs (rosemary, thyme)
Use for: Venison, elk, wild boar (marinate 4-24 hours)

Citrus Marinade for Poultry
- 1/4 cup orange juice
- 2 tbsp lime juice
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tbsp honey
- 1 tsp ground cumin
Use for: Quail, pheasant, duck (marinate 2-6 hours)

Spicy Cajun Rub
- 2 tbsp paprika
- 1 tbsp garlic powder
- 1 tbsp onion powder
- 1 tbsp oregano
- 1 tsp thyme
- 1 tsp cayenne pepper
Use for: Alligator, bison, wild boar

COOKING TIPS BY MEAT TYPE
=========================

Venison Cooking Tips
- Don't overcook - best served medium-rare to medium
- Marinate tough cuts for 4-24 hours
- Use moist cooking methods for shoulder and shank cuts
- Pair with fruit-based sauces to complement the flavor

Wild Boar Tips
- Cook thoroughly - internal temperature of 160°F minimum
- Benefits from slow cooking methods
- Pairs well with apples, pears, and root vegetables
- Fat content is higher than most game meats

Elk Guidelines
- Very lean - cook quickly or use moist heat
- Similar to beef but cook 25% less time
- Ground elk works well in any ground beef recipe
- Don't overcook steaks and chops

Bison Instructions
- Cook like beef but reduce cooking time by 1/3
- High protein, low fat content
- Best when not cooked past medium
- No need to marinate tender cuts

Exotic Poultry Notes
- Quail cooks very quickly - watch carefully
- Duck has high fat content - score the skin
- Pheasant can be dry - brine before cooking
- All poultry should reach 165°F internal temperature

Specialty Meats
- Kangaroo: Serve rare to medium-rare only
- Alligator: Similar to fish - don't overcook
- Rabbit: Benefits from moist cooking methods
- Yak: Cook like premium beef

FOOD SAFETY REMINDERS
=====================

Temperature Guidelines
- Ground meats: 160°F
- Whole muscle meats: 145°F + 3-minute rest
- Poultry: 165°F
- Always use a meat thermometer

Thawing Safety
- Thaw in refrigerator, never at room temperature
- Use thawed meat within 1-2 days
- Never refreeze completely thawed meat
- Cold water thawing: change water every 30 minutes

Cross-Contamination Prevention
- Use separate cutting boards for raw meat
- Wash hands for 20 seconds after handling raw meat
- Clean all surfaces with sanitizing solution
- Store raw meat on bottom shelf of refrigerator
